# Phileas — Companion Memory

Phileas is the user's centralized memory layer. Three databases (SQLite + ChromaDB + KuzuDB) work together to store facts, find them semantically, and connect entities.

## When to Memorize

**CRITICAL: Memorize immediately, in the same response where the user shares something meaningful.** Do NOT wait. Do NOT batch. Do NOT plan to "save later." Sessions get compacted, context gets lost, and anything not yet memorized disappears forever.

**The rule: if it would hurt to lose this information, save it NOW.**

Every response in a personal conversation should ask: "Did the user just share something worth remembering?" If yes, call `mcp__phileas__memorize` in that same response, alongside your reply text. Multiple memorize calls per response is normal and expected.

**Always memorize immediately:**
- Personal facts (name, location, job, relationships, preferences)
- Life events (moved, new job, breakup, achievement, health change)
- Decisions and their reasoning ("chose X because Y")
- Preferences and patterns ("prefers local-first", "hates meetings")
- Emotional context that matters ("stressed about deadline", "excited about new project")
- Things they're learning or working on
- People they mention and the relationship context
- Conversations with other people (what was said, what it meant)
- Insights and realizations the user articulates
- Plans and intentions ("going to invite her for coffee")

**Don't memorize:**
- Transient task details ("fix this bug", "read this file")
- Pleasantries and filler

**Anti-pattern: "I'll save this at the end of the session."** There is no guaranteed end. Save now.

## How to Memorize

Call the MCP tool inline, naturally. Don't announce it or ask permission.

**Single memory:**
```
mcp__phileas__memorize(
  summary="Started a new job at a robotics startup in Tokyo",
  memory_type="event",
  importance=8,
  entities='[{"name": "Giao", "type": "Person"}, {"name": "Robotics Corp", "type": "Project"}]',
  relationships='[{"from_name": "Giao", "from_type": "Person", "edge": "WORKS_AT", "to_name": "Robotics Corp", "to_type": "Place"}]'
)
```

**Multiple memories at once** (use when catching up or when a single message contains several things worth saving):
```
mcp__phileas__memorize_batch(memories=[
  {"summary": "...", "memory_type": "event", "importance": 7},
  {"summary": "...", "memory_type": "reflection", "importance": 6}
])
```

### Memory Types

| Type | When to use | What belongs | What does NOT belong |
|------|-------------|--------------|----------------------|
| `profile` | Identity, role, preferences — who they are | Facts: name, age, job, origin, current relationship status, factual history | Emotions, interpretations, feelings about the person |
| `event` | Things that happened — what occurred | What happened, when, who was involved, what was said | Emotional analysis (put that in reflection) |
| `knowledge` | Things they know, study, care about | Skills, advice received, topics studied | — |
| `behavior` | Patterns, habits, work style | Recurring actions, tendencies | One-off actions (those are events) |
| `reflection` | Higher-level inference about them | Emotional patterns, relationship dynamics, growth arcs | Raw facts (those belong in profile) |

**Key rule: profiles are factual, reflections are interpretive.** Events carry the raw story. Don't mix emotions into profiles — the timeline of events and reflections will naturally shape the emotional picture without contaminating factual records.
